# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A single finished slip-cast ceramic Alto C ocarina — the classic rounded "sweet potato" vessel-flute silhouette, a smooth closed cavity that swells in the middle and tapers to a short stub mouthpiece at one end. The recognizable features that read it as an ocarina: a small fipple mouthpiece with a flat blown end, a rectangular voicing window with a clean sharp labium edge cut into the upper body just past the mouthpiece, and a scatter of round finger holes of varying sizes across the rounded belly with no bore tube running through it. The body is Cone 6 stoneware with an exterior-only satin glaze that leaves the voicing window, finger-hole rims, and mouth interior as bare matte clay; the surface shows the soft hand-finished roundness of a cast-and-tuned ceramic object. Scale cues read as a palm-sized hand instrument. Clean neutral light-grey studio background, soft directional studio light grazing across the glazed curve to show form, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The slip-cast ceramic ocarina resting on a maker's bench in a small ceramics workshop, staged among the workflow that produced it — a sealed PLA 3D-printed master, a two-piece plaster mother mold, and a leather-hard greenware blank set nearby, suggesting the digital-master to fired-vessel pipeline. Warm natural window light, a kiln and tuner just out of focus in the background, shallow depth of field keeping the finished glazed ocarina sharp. Concept visualization only, environmental storytelling about a ceramic vessel-flute being tuned by hand.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the fipple voicing mechanism: the flat blown mouthpiece end, the narrow windway channel, and the rectangular voicing window where the air sheet strikes a crisp sharp labium edge cut into the stoneware body. Raking low-angle light emphasizes the sharpness of the labium edge, the smoothness of the windway, and the contrast between the satin exterior glaze and the bare matte clay left unglazed inside the window and around the hole rims. High detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts or measurement lines; no impossible mechanisms; no straight bore tube or transverse-flute body (this is a closed Helmholtz vessel, not a pipe); anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
